About

I currently work as a Postdoctoral Researcher in the ISCycle project at the School of Allied Health, University of Limerick, Ireland. This project is generously funded by the Sustainable Energy Authority of Ireland (SEAI) and the Department of Transport. I also held a part-time position as an instructor in the Department of Civil Engineering (Fall 2023) at the University of Limerick. My research interests revolve around the comprehensive exploration of human choice behavior using econometric and machine learning methodologies. I am dedicated to advancing these methods to enhance our understanding of how individuals and households make complex decisions related to their daily activity patterns.

I obtained my PhD in transportation engineering in 2023 from Imperial College London, UK, with a dissertation titled "Endogeneity and Consideration Set Issues in Residential Location Choice Models." Before that, I gained valuable experience working at the Center for Transportation Research from 2016 to 2019 while simultaneously pursuing a Master of Science in Engineering with a specialization in Transportation Engineering in 2018 at The University of Texas at Austin. I also have a Bachelor of Technology degree in Civil Engineering, which I earned in 2016 from the Indian Institute of Technology, Bombay. I have been fortunate to participate in a semester exchange program at the Cooper Union for the Advancement of Science and Art, New York.

I am a Imperial College Global Fellow awardee [2022] and Turing Scheme research awardee [2022], which allowed me to conduct research at the Technical University of Munich (TUM), Germany. I have also received Department Dixon Scholarship and Wellcome Trust Scholarship for Ph.D. in UK [2019-2023], Texas District Student Fellowship [2017-2018] and Graduate fellowship to undertake graduate studies at UT Austin [2016-2018].

Words to live by . . .

What a misfortune, although you are made
for fine and great works
this unjust fate of yours always
denies you encouragement and success;
that base customs should block you;
and pettiness and indifference.
And how terrible the day when you yield
(the day when you give up and yield),
and you leave on foot for Susa,
and you go to the monarch Artaxerxes
who favorably places you in his court,
and offers you satrapies and the like.
And you accept them with despair
these things that you do not want.
Your soul seeks other things, weeps for other things;
> the praise of the public and the Sophists,
the hard-won and inestimable Well Done;
the Agora, the Theater, and the Laurels.
How can Artaxerxes give you these,
where will you find these in a satrapy;
and what life can you live without these.

The Satrapy, by Constantine P. Cavafy
